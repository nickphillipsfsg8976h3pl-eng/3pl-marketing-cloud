3 ways EdTech can help with classroom management and student behaviour
 
  1. Differentiated and personalised learning: It's no surprise that challenging behaviour arises when students feel like they are outside their comfort zone. But balancing the needs of the curriculum and each individual student's ability level is no mean feat. With EdTech, students are placed on their own individual pathway and content is tailored for them so they are always working in their zone of Proximal Development.
  2. Engaging and rewarding activities: Activities in our programs draw on best practices when it comes to gamified learning. Your students will be having so much fun, they won't even realise how much extra practice they are doing. Plus, with rewards and certificates for effort, your students will always feel like they are progressing.
  3. Automation: It'll take care of your busy work like marking, so you can spend more time focusing on those tricky students that need more support.
