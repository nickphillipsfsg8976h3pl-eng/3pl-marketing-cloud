1. Define your culture focus

  • The insight: "Culture is just a collective noun for behaviours. There are behaviours we encourage, there are behaviours we tolerate."
  • Your action: List 3 behaviours you currently tolerate that you'd like to move to the ‘encourage pile’. Start with just one this week.

2. Try the P3P3F3 method:

  • The framework: Past, Present, Future – 3 minutes each for restorative conversations.
  • Your action:
    • Past (3 minutes): "What happened?" – Focus on engagement, not investigation.
    • Present (3 minutes): "How did that make people feel?" – Build empathy & awareness.
    • Future (3 minutes): "What will you do to fix this?" – Create ownership & action.
  • The goal: Get students thanked and congratulated for taking personal responsibility.

3. Shift from Traffic Light to Roundabout thinking:

  • The insight: Ask yourself – am I doing all the work while students just comply (traffic light) or am I teaching them to navigate skilfully (roundabout)?
  • Your action: Identify one area where you could hand over more responsibility to students this week.
