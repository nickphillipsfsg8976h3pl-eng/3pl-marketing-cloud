Here's a quick recap for you!


The culture-first approach: Adam's core message was clear: "Whether you're a school leader or a classroom teacher, your most important job is to lead the culture." When we get this right, "everything follows."


The fundamental shift: Culture is simply "a collective noun for behaviours" – behaviours we encourage vs behaviours we tolerate. Your job is to move behaviours from the tolerate pile to the encourage pile.

The Traffic Light vs Roundabout framework


Traffic Light schools: Authority does all the work, students just comply (or cheat).
Roundabout schools: Shared responsibility where students learn to navigate the system.


The Indiana study Adam shared showed dramatic results when 265 intersections switched to roundabouts:

  • 90% reduction in fatalities
  • 70% reduction in collisions
  • 50% increase in traffic flow.

The lesson? "Harness the energy of our young people and allow them to learn how to use the system rather than continually catch them doing the wrong thing."


Your implementation toolkit: The P3P3F3 method

Adam demonstrated exactly how to have restorative conversations that build personal responsibility:


Past (3 minutes): "What happened?" – Focus on engagement, not investigation.
Present (3 minutes): "How did that make people feel?" – Build empathy and awareness.
Future (3 minutes): "What will you do to fix this?" – Create ownership and action.


The goal: Get students thanked and congratulated for taking personal responsibility. This creates a school culture where students self-report problems because they know they'll be helped to resolve them, not punished.
