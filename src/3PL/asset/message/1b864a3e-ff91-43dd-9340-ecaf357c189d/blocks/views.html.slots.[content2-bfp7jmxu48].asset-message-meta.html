In Adam Voigt's EdChat, he challenged us to move beyond compliance and develop students who actively participate and take ownership of their learning.


His ‘roundabout’ approach was all about teaching students to navigate systems skilfully rather than just follow rules. This got us thinking about engagement in other areas of learning – particularly reading.


Adam talked about the difference between students who simply comply and those who truly participate. We see this same distinction in reading instruction:

  • Compliant readers: Decode words when asked, complete assignments, sit quietly during reading time.
  • Engaged readers: Choose books independently, discuss stories enthusiastically, see reading as enjoyable rather than just required.

The Science of Reading research shows us how to cultivate that second type – the engaged learners Adam described.


We've put together a comprehensive guide that explores how evidence‑based reading instruction transforms students from passive participants into genuinely engaged learners.

Key Insights include:

  • The role of success and mastery in creating intrinsic motivation
  • Digital approaches that personalise learning and foster independence
  • Moving from external compliance to internal drive for learning.

As the research shows: ‘Encouraging a genuine love of reading can transform students into not only proficient readers but also passionate and engaged learners.'


When students feel competent and confident in their abilities, they're more likely to engage authentically in all areas of learning – supporting the positive culture you're working to build.
