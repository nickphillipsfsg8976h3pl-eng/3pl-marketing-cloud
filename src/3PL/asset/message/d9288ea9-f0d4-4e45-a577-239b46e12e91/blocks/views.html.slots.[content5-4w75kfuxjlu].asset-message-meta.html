Where can I get decodable texts?

 

The term decodable indicates that words in the text can be decoded using the phonics skills and GPCs children are learning. For example, if children are focusing on the s, a, t, p sounds, the matching books only use these sounds alongside some high-frequency words like the and a. A sentence would be: Pat sat. Decodable readers typically start with simple, easily decodable words and gradually increase in complexity as the reader progresses in their phonics skills.
