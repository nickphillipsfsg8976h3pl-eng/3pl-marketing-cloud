What is systematic, synthetic phonics?

 

Queensland’s teachers are being asked to focus on systematic, synthetic phonics instruction. This is because research shows this focus boosts students' results and closes the achievement gap in reading fluency.

 

Systematic refers to the sequence of Grapheme-Phoneme Correspondences (GPCs).

Under Department guidelines, teaching also needs to be systematic, with regular and consistent instruction, and structured lessons that include explicit instruction.

 

Synthetic phonics involves identifying sets of letters and sounds, blending the sounds all the way though the word, and then segmenting the word into sounds to spell it.

 

Fast Phonics is the systematic, synthetic phonics program that provides a strong foundation for students’ reading skills and confidence. Fast Phonics clearly and explicitly teaches phonics systematically through instructional videos and guided practice activities (both online and print are provided).
