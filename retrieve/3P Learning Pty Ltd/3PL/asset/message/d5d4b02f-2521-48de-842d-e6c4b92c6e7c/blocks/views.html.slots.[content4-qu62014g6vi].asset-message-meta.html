We are pleased to announce a new report that shows the relationship between the Brightpath Maths assessments and the curriculum.

This new report, which complements the existing suite of Maths reports, will help you more readily view the range of ability in your class relative to the skills and understandings assessed in the tests, and the curriculum.

Brightpath Curriculum Report

 
 
 

The report is available for all previously completed assessments. To view it, log in to Brightpath, select View reports, and look for Curriculum reports.

The report currently shows v8.1 of the Australian Curriculum, with support for the new Western Australian Maths Curriculum coming in 2026.

Background: The Brightpath Maths assessments have been developed to assess a continuum of development so that you can see where your students are in their learning and what they need to learn next. As such the Maths assessments assess a wide range of ability in a given year level, and some of the questions assess skills typically associated with aspects of the curriculum that are below a student's year level and some questions assess skills associated with aspects of the curriculum that are above that student's year level.

The report therefore links studentsâ€™ results to relevant curriculum content from the year levels targeted by the test, as well as the years above and below.

Cautions: It is important to be aware that not all aspects of the Maths curriculum can be assessed through multiple-choice and short answers questions. It is also important to note that whilst questions can be matched to skills described in the curriculum, the fact that students answered a question or questions right does not mean that the students have fully mastered the skills described in the curriculum.

As with all test data teachers need to use their own assessments and observations when deciding whether students are ready to move onto new content or whether they need further teaching and consolidation.
