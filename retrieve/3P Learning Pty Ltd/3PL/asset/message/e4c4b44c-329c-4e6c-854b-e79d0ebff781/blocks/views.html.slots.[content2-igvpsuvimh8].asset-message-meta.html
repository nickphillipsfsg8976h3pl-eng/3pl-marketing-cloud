Why are exit slips the triple threat of formative assessments?

 
  1. Using exit slips after introducing new material or at the end of a topic helps students reflect on what they've learnt and consolidate their knowledge.
  2. As far as formative assessments go, exit slips are one of the quickest and easiest ways to check your students' understanding.
  3. Not only can you use them to check for understanding and skills gained, but you can also use them to revise future instructional strategies. For example, you may ask students what they liked about doing group work and what ways they felt like it could be improved.
  4. Bonus: it's relatively low-stress for the students and can help promote a sense of collaborative learning. For example, if you ask for feedback, it can help your students feel like they have some ownership over their learning and motivate them to reflect on how they learn best.
