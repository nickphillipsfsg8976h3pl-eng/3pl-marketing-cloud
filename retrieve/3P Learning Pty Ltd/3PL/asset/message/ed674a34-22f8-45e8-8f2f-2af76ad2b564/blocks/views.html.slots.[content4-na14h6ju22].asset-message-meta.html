What is explicit instruction?

 

NSWâ€™s teachers are being asked to focus on systematic and explicit teaching because it has been shown to boost student results and close the achievement gap. The NSW Department of Education mandated explicit phonics instruction in teaching in 2023.

 

For phonics instruction, this means teaching Grapheme-Phoneme Correspondences (GPCs) in a clear, structured sequence. Under the guidelines of the NSW Department of Education, the teaching needs to be regular and consistent, follow a researched sequence, and with structured lessons that include explicit instruction.

 

Sequence of grapheme-phoneme correspondences

 

The NSW Syllabus includes a suggested sequence for introducing GPCs. (Note: this sequence is not mandated.)

 

ABC Reading Eggs and Fast Phonics both follow a researched sequence that closely aligns to the intent of the NSW Syllabus. Each program begins with single grapheme-phoneme correspondences and builds to more complex correspondence and skills. The goal is to help children identify and read the greatest number of GPCs quickly and provide a reliable foundation for future acquisition of reading skills.
