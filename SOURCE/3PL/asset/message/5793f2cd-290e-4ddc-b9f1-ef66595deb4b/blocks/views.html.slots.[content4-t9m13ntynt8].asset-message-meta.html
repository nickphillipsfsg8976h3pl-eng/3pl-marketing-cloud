The challenge Adam posed

Most educators are overwhelmed trying to manage everything. Adam cut through the noise with one clear message: "Whether you're a school leader or a classroom teacher, your most important job is to lead the culture." When you get this right, "everything follows."

The framework you'll learn
  • Traffic lights = Authority controls everything, people comply or cheat.
  • Roundabouts = Shared responsibility, people learn to navigate skilfully.

Real data from Indiana: When 265 traffic lights became roundabouts, they saw 90% fewer fatalities and 50% more efficient flow. The education parallel? "Harness the energy of our young people and allow them to learn how to use the system rather than continually catch them doing the wrong thing."


The P3P3F3 method for behaviour – Adam demonstrated a practical 9-minute framework for restorative conversations:

  • Past (3 minutes): "What happened?" (Focus on engagement, not investigation).
  • Present (3 minutes): "How did that make people feel?" (Build empathy).
  • Future (3 minutes): "What will you do to fix this?" (Create ownership).

The result? Students who take personal responsibility and develop internal motivation instead of external compliance.


Schools implementing these approaches report:
  • Dramatic reductions in suspensions and behaviour referrals
  • Students self-reporting problems because they trust the resolution process.

Adam shared the story of a student who couldn't sleep until she confessed to graffiti – not from fear, but because "she was developing a conscience."
