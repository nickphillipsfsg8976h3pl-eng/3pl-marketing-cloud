Over the past 20 years, average reading performance has been stagnant for Year 5 students with fewer high-achieving readers among the cohort than there were in 2001 or 2005. Student achievement data and increased research on how children learn to read have sparked a growing movement toward evidence-based practices.
 

New Zealand is committed to teaching and learning underpinned by the science of learning, and this is evident in the refreshed Te Mātaiaho | The New Zealand Curriculum, set to roll out in 2025. For reading in Phase 1 this includes systematic, explicit instruction to develop foundational reading skills and empower students to love reading.

 

As with every new curriculum, we know there’s pressure on teachers to understand what’s new and what changes are required—which is precisely why you can count on Reading Eggs.

 

Committed to both evidence-based practice and supporting teachers, stay tuned over the next three weeks to learn about how Reading Eggs uses systematic explicit teaching, and how this works with a structured literacy approach to help students achieve reading success.
 

You'll also find tips on where to find ready-to-use resources, books and in-class activities to support every learner. Plus, we’d like to invite you to a free webinar in November on using Reading Eggs and the highly impactful Fast Phonics series of lessons in your classroom.
